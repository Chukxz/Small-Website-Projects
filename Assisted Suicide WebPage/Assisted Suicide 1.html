Should Assisted Suicide Be Legal?

Assisted suicide is not a new thing. It started with the Death with Dignity Act or Measure 16, of 1994. This Act was a citizen's lead initiative by Barbara Coombs Lee a nurse, lawyer and the chief petitioner of Measure 16. And Dr. Peter Goodwin a family practitioner and professor who drafted the measure. We all know that suicide is the ending of one's life. but assisted suicide is more distinctive. assisted suicide is the help provided by a doctor by the request of a patient, to end his or her life.

With that being said, assisted suicide is often grouped together with euthanasia under MAiD ❲Medical‐aid‐in dying❳. However they do not mean the same thing as euthanasia is deliberately ending a person's life to relieve suffering, while assisted suicide is suicide effected with the assistance of another person, especially the taking of lethal drugs provided by a doctor for the purpose by a patient suffering from a terminal illness or incurable condition. There are different views on assisted suicide. The question of whether assisted suicide should be legal or not depends on public opinion. One may be tempted to ask why assisted suicide should exist in the first place. It is no wonder evidence suggests that the desire to hasten death often comes from wanting to maintain some power over one's life.

In addition, supporters of assisted suicide believe that patients on the verge of dying should be allowed to die with dignity. They claim that patients that are terminally ill should be allowed the right of choosing how they die. As choosing the right to assisted suicide would be the final exercise of autonomy for the dying. Contrary to their belief, the opposers believe that allowing assisted suicide devalues human life and violates certain religious and spiritual beliefs. They insinuate that legalising assisted suicide by attempting to establish an absolute right to bodily autonomy may undermine the importance and value of human life and humans as a whole. Also if a case of assisted suicide occurs in a country, state, province, region, or place where it has not been legalized then the doctor may be arrested. The patient may not also be in his or or her right mind and with further encouragement may change his or her mind.

Despite the fact that there exists some moral, ethnic, religious, and social contemplations on the issue, some countries have legalised assisted suicide. The countries which have legalised assisted suicide include Austria, Belgium, Canada, Germany, Luxembourg, the Netherlands, New Zealand, Spain, Switzerland, parts of the USA ❲ten states and the District of Columbia❳ and parts of Australia ❲all the states except New South Wales❳.

In summary, assisted suicide should be legal in the eyes of some as long as the patients have access to their autonomy, that is, their cognitive functions are working properly otherwise it will be a case of euthanasia. But in other people's opinions, it should be discouraged.
